【文献标题】: Enhancing Antimicrobial Peptide Function Prediction via Knowledge Transfer on Protein Language Models
【发表年份】: 2025
【S2 引用次数】: 1
【PDF 直链】: 

【摘要】: The excessive use of antibiotics has contributed to the growing resistance of various microbial pathogens, prompting the exploration of alternatives such as antimicrobial peptides (AMPs). However, wet-lab experimental methods for AMP identification are not only time-consuming but also resource-intensive. Machine learning (ML) and deep learning (DL) techniques have been increasingly employed for AMP classification to overcome these limitations. Recently, pre-trained protein language models (pLMs) have emerged, demonstrating their exceptional ability to represent biological sequences in a variety of prediction tasks. Building on these advancements, we present KT-AMPpred, a method for predicting antimicrobial peptides and their specific antimicrobial properties using transfer learning and fine-tuning techniques based on pre-trained protein language models. Utilizing transfer learning, KT-AMPpred effectively integrates knowledge from prior AMP classification tasks to improve the prediction of distinct antimicrobial properties. Additionally, the fine-tuning of pLM within KT-AMPpred further enhances its performance on downstream prediction tasks. The experimental outcomes reveal that KT-AMPpred exceeds current leading approaches on benchmark datasets, with visual analysis confirming its robust feature extraction capabilities, establishing it as an effective tool for antimicrobial peptide prediction.
